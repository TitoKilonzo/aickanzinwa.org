Our History

A Brief History of the Project

Africa Inland Church Kanzinwa is situated in Kanzinwa village, Mbarani Sub-location, Katse Location, Mumoni Sub-county, Mwingi North Constituency, Kitui County, Kenya.

On 8th November 2008, Africa Inland Church signed their first partnership agreement with Compassion International Kenya to holistically release the neediest children and youth from poverty in Jesus' Name. Operations officially commenced in January 2009.

2008
Partnership Established
AIC Kanzinwa signs first partnership agreement with Compassion International Kenya.
2009
Operations Begin
Centre officially commences operations with 200 registered beneficiaries under the Child Development Sponsorship Programme.
2024
AIC Maeni Satellite Launched
Recruitment drive for AIC Maeni Satellite selects 30 beneficiaries, extending reach to the most vulnerable families.
2025
371+ Beneficiaries
Programme grows to 371 registered beneficiaries under the Integrated Programme Module covering survival, home-based, and sponsorship programmes.
What Drives Us

Mission, Vision & Values

Mission Statement

Africa Inland Church Kanzinwa Child Development Centre exists to provide a child-friendly environment for holistic growth through community mobilisation, capacity building, and empowerment training.

Vision Statement

To empower less fortunate children and youth in the society — equipping them with the skills, confidence, and character to thrive beyond poverty.

Stewardship

Responsible management of every resource entrusted to us.

Integrity

Honesty and transparency in all our dealings and programmes.

Dignity

Affirming the inherent worth and value of every child we serve.

Excellence

Pursuing the highest standards in every programme and activity.

Transparency

Open, accountable reporting to all stakeholders and donors.

Passion

Deep commitment to the children and communities we serve.

Accountability

Full accountability to God, our partners, and our beneficiaries.

Compassion

Meeting needs with empathy, generosity, and Christ-like love.

Four Pillars

Holistic Development Areas

Our model addresses the whole child — spirit, livelihood, relationships, and body — with measurable outcomes for each area.

Spiritual Development

Demonstrating commitment to the Lordship of Christ — nurturing faith, values, and purpose in every registered child and youth.

Church-centred programming

Economic Development

Exhibiting motivation and skills to become economically self-supporting through vocational training, entrepreneurship, and scholarships.

Vocational & skills training

Socio-emotional Development

Interacting with other people in a healthy and compassionate manner — counselling, child protection, and community engagement.

Community & family focus

Physical Development

Choosing good health practices and being physically healthy — through medical outreach, nutrition support, and sports activities.

Health & nutrition support

"Our pertinent mission is to release one child or youth from poverty in Jesus' Name at a time, thus holistically developing them as everyone attains 22 years old — our maximum age of support."

— AIC Kanzinwa CYDC Mission Statement
